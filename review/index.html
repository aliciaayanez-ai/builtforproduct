8-Second Website Review

Verdict: Passes the 8-second test

Within 8 seconds, a visitor knows what the app is, who it's for, and why it's different. The headline does in 4 words what most startup sites can't do in 40.

0-2s
"No Fluff. Just Gains."

Opinionated, clear, memorable. Immediately repels the wrong user and attracts the right one.

2-5s
"Track your progressive overload, log every set, and see exactly how strong you're getting."

Specific. No jargon that isn't gym jargon. A lifter reads this and immediately knows: this is for me.

5-8s
One CTA: "Join the Waitlist"

One door. No confusion. The visitor knows exactly what to do next.

Opportunity 01

No social proof yet

A single quote from a beta tester does more than another feature card. People trust other people, not product pages.

Opportunity 02

No number above the fold

One number creates an anchor. "500 lifters on the waitlist" signals momentum and turns "launching soon" into evidence.

Opportunity 03

The design philosophy is the differentiator — elevate it

"Optimized for one-handed use between sets" is the moment a serious lifter decides this app was built by someone who gets it.
